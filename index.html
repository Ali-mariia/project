About Corona Virus

COVID-19, caused by the coronavirus SARS-CoV-2, is a highly contagious disease that emerged in late 2019. It primarily spreads through respiratory droplets and close contact. Common symptoms include fever, cough, and difficulty breathing, though some individuals may remain asymptomatic. Preventive measures such as wearing masks, maintaining social distance, frequent handwashing, and vaccination have been critical in controlling its spread. The pandemic significantly impacted global health, economies, and daily life, highlighting the importance of healthcare preparedness and scientific research.

Coronavirus what it is?

Coronavirus is a family of viruses that cause illnesses in humans and animals, ranging from the common cold to severe diseases like SARS, MERS, and COVID-19. It spreads primarily through respiratory droplets and contact with infected surfaces.

CORONA VIRUS SYMTOMS

COVID-19 symptoms include fever, cough, shortness of breath, fatigue, and loss of taste or smell, among others..

Coughing

Coughing is a natural reflex that helps clear the airways of irritants, mucus, or foreign particles. It can be triggered by infections, allergies, or environmental factors like smoke.

Hot Fever

A hot fever is a high body temperature, usually due to infection, helping the body fight illness. It can indicate an underlying health issue and may require medical attention if persistent.

Strong Headache

A headache is pain or discomfort in the head, scalp, or neck. It can be caused by various factors, such as stress, dehydration, or illness.

Shortness Of Breath

Shortness of breath is a feeling of difficulty or discomfort in breathing. It can be caused by various conditions, including respiratory infections, allergies, or heart problems.

Confusion

Confusion is a state of mental disorientation, where a person may have difficulty thinking clearly, making decisions, or understanding their surroundings.

Sore Throat

A sore throat is pain, irritation, or scratchiness in the throat, often caused by infections like colds or flu, or irritants like smoke. It can make swallowing painful or difficult..

How to Protect Yourself

To protect yourself from COVID-19, wear a mask, wash your hands regularly, and stay at home when possible.

Wear Mask

Wearing a mask helps prevent the spread of respiratory droplets, reducing the risk of transmitting or contracting infections like COVID-19.

Coronavirus Cases

COVID-19 cases refer to the number of individuals diagnosed with the coronavirus disease, either through symptoms or positive test results.

Read More

Cases 01

The first confirmed case of COVID-19 was reported in December 2019 in Wuhan, China. The patient, who was initially linked to a seafood market, exhibited symptoms similar to pneumonia, such as fever and cough. As the virus rapidly spread, it became clear that it was caused by a novel coronavirus, later named SARS-CoV-2. The disease, referred to as COVID-19, soon led to a global pandemic, with cases reported worldwide.

Read More

Cases 02

The second case of COVID-19 refers to the second individual diagnosed with the disease in a specific area or region. This can indicate the early stages of an outbreak, where the virus has started to spread to more individuals after the initial case. The second case is often closely monitored to determine how the virus is transmitted and whether it is spreading locally. In many instances, contact tracing and quarantining are done to prevent further infections

Read More

Cases 03

The third case of COVID-19 refers to the third confirmed instance of the disease in a particular area or among a group of individuals. In the early stages of the pandemic, as the virus spread globally, the third case in many regions often raised concerns about the virus's increasing presence. Early cases typically triggered public health investigations, contact tracing, and increased awareness about preventive measures like social distancing, mask-wearing, and hygiene practices.

HOSPITALS PROVIDE COVID VACATION..

AGHA KHAN UNIVERSITY HOSPITAL

Aga Khan University Hospital (AKUH) in Karachi has been actively involved in COVID-19 vaccination efforts. They have conducted clinical trials, including a mix-and-match study of COVID-19 vaccines, assessing the safety and effectiveness of combining different vaccine types.
For those seeking vaccination, AKUH has established a dedicated COVID-19 Vaccination Centre at their Stadium Road campus.To schedule an appointment, individuals should call AKUH's helpline at +92 21 111 911 911

LIAQUAT NATIONAL

Liaquat National Hospital and Medical College in Karachi has been actively involved in COVID-19 vaccination efforts. They have established one of Karachi's largest adult vaccination centers to facilitate the vaccination process.
For the most current information on COVID-19 vaccination services, including eligibility, scheduling, and any updates, please contact the hospital directly at (021) 111-456-456 or visit their official website.

HASHMANI HOSPITAL SADDAR

Hashmanis Hospital in Saddar, Karachi, offers COVID-19 vaccination services, including the Sputnik V vaccine. Vaccination is available at their Numaish branch.
For more information or to register for vaccination, you can visit their official website. Additionally, you can obtain your vaccination certificate online by visiting the National Immunization Management System (NIMS) portal

SIUT

The Sindh Institute of Urology and Transplantation (SIUT) in Karachi has been actively involved in COVID-19 vaccination efforts. They have conducted webinars discussing the ethical considerations of mandatory vaccinations for healthcare workers. SIUT
For COVID-19 vaccination registration, individuals can send an SMS with their Computerized National Identity Card (CNIC) number to 1166 or visit the National Immunization Management System (NIMS) website.
